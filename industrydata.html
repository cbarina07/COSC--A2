Industry Data

What are the job titles for our group's ideal jobs? How do each of these rank in terms of demand from employers?

The overarching job titles for our group's ideal career paths include are Front-End Developer/Web Developer, Criminal Intelligence Analyst, Developer Programmer, Quality Assurance Tester, Software Engineer and Cloud Consultant and Data Analyst. According to Labour Insight Jobs (Burning Glass Technologies, March 2018), there have been 738 job postings for front end developers which ranks 6th, 278 job postings for developer programmers which ranks 23rd and 539 job postings for software engineers which ranks 11th (all are ranked within the IT category). According to SEEK a jobsite used to advertise open job availability, as of the 7th of September 2022, there are currently 75 job postings for Criminal Intelligence Analysts, 2632 job postings for Quality Assurance Testers and 23 job postings for Cloud Consultants.

What set of skills general and IT specific are required for the jobs?

Employers looking for front-end developers and web developers value the general skills of good communication, problem-solving, leadership skills to take accountability over projects, teamwork. In terms of IT specific skills, coding languages such as Python, JavaScript and its modern frameworks such as Vue, React and Node are highly sought after. Furthermore, exceptional skills in HTML 5, CSS, LESS/SASS, data structures and algorithms and cloud computing are required. The consensus of skills for a criminal intelligence analyst includes communication, advanced research and analytic skills, teamwork, critical thinking, problem-solving, leadership and coaching skills. Some technical and IT specific skills include database management through languages such as SQL, cybersecurity, technical knowledge in the justice system and law enforcement, data analytics and data science skills. As a developer programmer general key skill requirements include: mathematical expertise, problem-solving, organisation, time-management, teamwork, troubleshooting and detail oriented. Moreover, IT skill requirements include: a plethora of coding languages including Python, JavaScript, C/C++ among others; SQL, data structures and algorithms, HTML and CSS, Source Control Management (SCM), cloud computing. General skills for a quality assurance tester consist of good communication skills, adequate time management skills, strong attention to detail, teamwork and presentation skills. For IT specific skills, quality assurance game testers would need to have skills in digital/graphic design (Adobe CC), animation and 3D design skills (Maya, Mudbox), databases and SQL, programming knowledge (Python, Java). As for a software engineer, employers of this job look for the general skills of communication, teamwork, multitasking, problem-solving and attention to detail. In some cases leadership skills are also sought after. For specialised IT skills, software engineers would need to be skilful in a multitude of programming languages such as Python, Java, JavaScript, C#, C++, HTML, CSS, PHP and Perl. Skills in database management and SQL are also required. Key cloud consultant general skills include excellent customer service and communication, problem-solving, analytical, collaborative skills, ability to build relationships and detail oriented. Furthermore, some IT specific skills range from having skills in network administration like DNS, VPN and BGP; to having adequate programming skills in NodeJS, Python and Java.

How do the IT specific skills in your required skill set rank in terms of demand from employers?

Industry required IT specific skills ranking in group:
1. JavaScript
2. Structured Query Language (SQL)
3. Python, Java, C/C++
4. HTML, CSS, C#, cloud computing
5. 3D design (Maya, MudBox)
6. Node, LESS/SASS, PHP, Perl, DNS, VPN and BGP knowledge.

In the report conducted by Burning Glass Technologies (March 2018) regarding the ‘Skills in Greatest Demand’, the top 3 skills sought after by employees were SQL, JavaScript and JAVA. Upon researching the relevant jobs of each group member, all 3 of these skills were a key requirement for multiple jobs. JavaScript was the most prominent IT specific skill across all our group members’ ideal jobs and the second most sought after skill in the research program conducted. Following this, SQL was the next most recurring skill in our teams’ ideal jobs, which paralleled the study’s ranked one most demanded skill. Thus, in terms of demand from employers SQL, JavaScript and Java are seen to be the most sought-after skills in the industry as supported by our own groups ideal jobs skill requirements. Going down the list, graphic design was the next highest skill within our collective skill requirements, ranking at 10th followed by Microsoft C# ranked at 12th. Although this skill’s position ranking was much lower than the rest, the actual difference in job postings between the positions of 1st and 10th was a mere 1,502 postings. Furthermore, when compared to the same study performed by Burning Glass Technologies in May 24, 2017, graphic design did not make it within the ranking, placing it out of the top 25 most specialised skills in the industry. As a result, our group’s IT specific skills mirrored the importance as the rankings proposed by the Burning Glass Technologies’ 2018 study to a degree; with new skills being in demand as the technology industry broadens in scope.

How do the general skills in your required skill set rank in terms of demand from employers?

Industry required general skills ranking in group:
1. Communication
2. Teamwork + Problem-solving
3. Detail-oriented
4. Leadership
5. Analytic + Time management
6. Coaching, mathematical expertise, troubleshooting, presentation, multitasking, customer service

After extensive research on the general skills necessary on job recruitment sites such as SEEK and Indeed, the majority of our groups’ ideal jobs requirements shared similar general skills paired with a few industry specific ones. The number one basic skill that all these careers had in common was – communication skills. The next ranked general skill in our group was a tie between teamwork and problem-solving skills, which appeared in almost all our ideal jobs. Most jobs nowadays require some form of teamwork or collaboration whether it be through group projects or discussions with clients, consequently an employee’s ability to work in a team effectively is favoured. Problem-solving skills were considered just as important as teamwork skills which is reasonable considering the majority of our jobs are IT based. Jobs such as programming and software engineering would require a great deal of problem-solving as certain tasks would require problems to be broken down into smaller ones in order to find the best effective solution.

Our third most common general skill in our ideal jobs was the ability to pay attention to detail as seen in the jobs as a programmer, quality assurance games tester, software engineer and cloud consultant. A quality assurance tester is a great example of a career that highly values people with this skill as they need someone that is capable of looking at the fine details of games and pinpoint any minor inconvenience that may be present before its release. Leadership skill is ranked 4th and analytic and time-management skills are tied at 5th; showing more value in certain positions in companies. Last in the rankings of general skills are mathematical expertise, troubleshooting, presentation, multitasking, customer service and coaching. These types of skills are mainly industry specific such as mathematical skills are highly sought after in programming and not necessary for front-end developers as they focus on the ‘looks’ of programs whilst programmers write the actual functions and code.

When comparing our collective skill requirements to ‘Skills in Greatest Demand’, a study conducted by Burning Glass Technologies (Burning Glass Technologies, February 2018), it was apparent that some skills were ranked vastly different. Communication skills remained a number 1 priority at ranked 1 alongside problem-solving skills which was placed 2nd in the studies ranking. Teamwork came at 5th and detail-oriented skills came 7th. This variance in rankings to our own could be an indication of requirements from broader jobs in the IT field, illustrating how vast the industry is and how different the skills may be in each profession.

What are the 3 highest ranked IT specific skills which are not in your required skill set?

3 of the highest ranked IT specific skills that are not required in our group’s skill set from Burning Glass Technologies (Burning Glass Technologies, February 2018) are Technical Support (Ranked 6th) which includes being able to assist people that have a problem with their technological devices. Business Management (Ranked 10th) which includes the ability to organise and coordinate business related actions and Customer Service (Ranked 13th) which includes dealing with customers in a positive manner. In comparison, technical support ranks 8th according to CompTIA (Kevkhishvili, 2022) while business management comes under business skills which is ranked 22nd. Furthermore, customer service comes under professional skills and is ranked 19th. These statistics highlight that even though these skills are not required, they may be useful in the future and will undoubtedly be a benefit for candidate selection.

What are the 3 highest ranked general skills which are not in your required skill set?

The three highest ranked general skills not required for our groups chosen career paths according to Burning Glass Technologies (Burning Glass Technologies, February 2018) are Organisational skills (Ranked 3rd), which include scheduling, managing time efficiently and knowing which tasks to accomplish and when, Writing (Ranked 4th) which includes the ability to write in a sophisticated, coherent and topigorical manner and lastly Creativity (Ranked 9th) which involves the ability to be consistently open-minded, curious and imaginative when faced with problems. In contrast, organisational skills ranks 16th according to Maryville University (Top 16 Skills Needed in 2020 and Beyond, n.d.) and comes under time management. Writing comes under written communication and ranks 7th while creativity ranks 9th.
